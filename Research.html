The Spencer Creek Historical Trail (SCHT) is a 2.5 km trail that stretches from the Ogilvie Street bridge and across Spencer Creek to the Mill Street bridge located in the heart of Dundas, Ontario.

The City of Hamilton has an important role to play in the enhancement of trails through digital technology. This includes providing funding, promoting the use of trails, and collaborating with other organizations and stakeholders. Additionally, the City must ensure safety and accessibility for all users, such as maintenance, safety features, and accessibility features for users with disabilities.



Hamilton Conservation Authority

The Hamilton Conservation Authority (HCA) is a public agency responsible for the management and conservation of natural resources, parks and recreational areas, and reducing the risk of natural hazards. It also promotes environmental education and awareness through programs and events, and serves as a steward of the natural environment, working to ensure the sustainable use and preservation of natural resources for present and future generations.

Bruce trail Conservancy

The Bruce Trail Conservancy is a non-profit organization dedicated to preserving and managing the 890-kilometer-long Bruce Trail along the Niagara Escarpment in Ontario. As Canada's largest land trust, it protects ecologically significant areas, maintains the trail, and promotes public access. Supported by volunteers, members, and donors, the conservancy safeguards biodiversity, offers outdoor recreation opportunities, and fosters environmental education

Dundas Valley Rotary Club

The Sunrise Rotary Club in Dundas is a vibrant and active organization that is involved in a wide range of initiatives and programs aimed at serving the needs of the local community. Some of the club's main areas of focus include supporting local charities, providing assistance to those in need, sponsoring youth programs, and organizing community events. In addition to these initiatives, the club also places a strong emphasis on fellowship and camaraderie among its members. Through regular meetings, social events, and other activities, members have the opportunity to build lasting friendships and develop valuable connections with others in the community.
The Spencer Creek Trail and the Town of Dundas play a crucial role in connecting the Dundas Valley Trails with the larger Cootes to Escarpment Park System. This network of trails and outdoor spaces provides opportunities for people of all ages and abilities to enjoy the natural beauty of the region and stay connected with their community. By supporting initiatives aimed at enhancing and expanding these trails and outdoor spaces, the Sunrise Rotary Club is helping to promote healthy aging, build community, and protect the natural environment for future generations to enjoy.

Introducing two esteemed members of the Sunrise Rotary Club

First, we have Dr. Brian Baetz, a Civil Engineer, dedicated community leader and a passionate advocate for nature and the outdoors.With a wealth of knowledge and experience in these areas, Brian has made significant contributions to the club and to the broader community, inspiring others to get involved in important initiatives aimed at enhancing the natural environment and promoting sustainable practices.

Joining Fraser Forrest, Fraser, a dedicated member of the Sunrise Rotary Club and a skilled Project Engineer with a background in Nuclear Engineering. Fraser has an impressive a history, having previously worked as an Aerospace Engineer in Manitoba before relocating to Dundas. Fraser is deeply committed to improving the natural beauty and accessibility of the Spencer Creek Trail, and his tireless efforts have made him a key advocate for the trail.Through his tireless efforts, Fraser has inspired others in the community to get involved in this important project, and his commitment to sustainability and preservation has helped to create a more vibrant and inclusive community.

Brian and Fraser, are passionate about re-imagining and developing the Spencer Creek Trail to make it more accessible and enjoyable for people of all ages and abilities. Both individuals have joined the Sunrise Rotary Club to collaborate with like-minded individuals and make a positive impact on the community. They recognize the importance of nature and outdoor spaces in promoting healthy aging and want to ensure that the trail remains a cherished local treasure for years to come.
